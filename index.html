01
Five symptoms. One root cause.
The problems listed are not separate issues. They are the same issue appearing in different places.
Symptom 01
Engineer diaries are not full. Utilisation is low and white space keeps reappearing despite efforts to fill it reactively.
Symptom 02
Quotes go out but conversion is inconsistent. There is no structured follow-up cadence in place to close the gap.
Symptom 03
The customer journey is patchy. The experience varies depending on which person handles the job, not on a system.
Symptom 04
Scaling feels chaotic. The current operational setup does not hold up as job volume increases.
Root Cause
Scheduling, quoting, and customer communications operate as separate functions rather than one connected operational engine. That is the gap. Fix the architecture, and all four symptoms resolve.

Layer 01 Diary Design - Engineer Scheduling System
What This Solves
Capacity rules built in advance
Define daily capacity per engineer by job type. Set hard limits before the week opens, not after white space appears.
Job-type blocking
Reserve morning slots for reactive work, afternoons for planned installs. Prevent one job type from cannibalising another.
Travel time baked in
Factor Bristol to Bath transit into each booking window. Eliminates late arrivals that trigger customer complaints.
Utilisation tracking by engineer
Know which engineers are consistently underbooked and why, before the problem compounds.
Recommended Tech Stack
GoHighLevel (GHL)
Primary CRM and calendar engine. Booking workflows, engineer diary management, and automated appointment reminders all live here. Source of truth for who is booked where.
Source of Truth / CRM
ClickUp
Internal ops workspace. Engineer capacity planning, job-type blocking rules, and scheduling SOPs live here as recurring tasks and templates. Integrates with GHL via Zapier.
Task and Ops Management
Zapier / Make
Automation layer. Connects GHL booking confirmations to ClickUp task creation and engineer notifications. No manual handoffs.
Automation Bridge
Google Sheets (transitional)
Weekly utilisation tracking until a live dashboard is built. Simple, visible, and shared with the ops team in week one.
Interim Reporting
Layer 02 Quote Engine - Conversion and Follow-Up System
What This Solves
Templated quote output
Every quote looks and reads the same. Consistent format signals professionalism and removes friction from the customer decision.
Automated 48-hour follow-up
If a quote is not accepted within 48 hours, a follow-up message fires automatically. No chasing by hand. No quotes falling through gaps.
Win and loss tracking per engineer
Know which engineers convert well and which do not. Identify the job types with the worst drop-off and address them with pricing or format changes.
Pipeline visibility
See open quotes, their value, and their age at a glance. No revenue disappearing silently because no one followed up.
Recommended Tech Stack
GoHighLevel (GHL)
Quote pipeline management, automated follow-up sequences, and deal stage tracking. Triggers the 48-hour follow-up SMS and email automatically when a quote is sent.
Primary Pipeline CRM
HubSpot (optional layer)
If MJP scales to multiple service lines or wants more granular deal reporting, HubSpot sits alongside GHL as a sales CRM with stronger analytics. GHL handles ops, HubSpot handles commercial pipeline.
Commercial CRM / Reporting
ClickUp
Quote review checklists, engineer quoting SOPs, and win/loss log templates. Each quote outcome is logged as a task with tags for job type, engineer, and outcome.
SOP and Logging Layer
Tradify / Jobber (field service)
Specialist quoting software built for electrical trades. Generates professional PDF quotes from job templates, integrates with GHL via webhook for follow-up automation.

Layer 03 Customer Journey - Consistent Experience System
What This Solves
Booking confirmation sequence
Automated SMS and email confirmation the moment a job is booked. Includes engineer name, arrival window, and what to have ready. Removes the "did anyone confirm this?" problem.
Pre-visit comms
Morning-of reminder with engineer name and photo. Sets expectations, reduces no-access jobs, and reflects the professionalism of 500 five-star reviews.
Post-job review prompt
Automated Google review request fires 2 hours after job marked complete. Protects the review count that MJP has built and compounds it going forward.
Consistent regardless of who handles the job
The customer experience is the same whether the office has two people or twelve. It runs on the system, not the individual.
Recommended Tech Stack
GoHighLevel (GHL)
Full customer journey automation. Booking confirmation, pre-visit reminder, post-job review request, and re-engagement sequences all built as GHL workflows. One platform handles the entire touchpoint chain.
Journey Automation Core
ClickUp
Customer journey SOP documentation. Each touchpoint is a checklist item so the team knows what fires automatically and what requires manual action. Onboards new staff in hours, not weeks.
SOP Documentation
Trustpilot / Google Business Profile
Review destination for post-job prompts. GHL sends the link directly via SMS. Maintains and grows the 500+ review bank that differentiates MJP in Bristol and Bath.
Reputation Management
WhatsApp Business (via GHL)
Pre-visit and day-of communications via WhatsApp for higher open rates. GHL handles WhatsApp messaging natively. Particularly effective for residential customers.
High-Open-Rate Comms

I designed and implemented this framework.
At an HVAC business facing the same operational setup, this system was successful and run end-to-end.
Rebuilt the scheduling system. Introduced capacity rules and job-type blocking that reduced white space within the first six weeks. Engineers moved from reactive gap-filling to structured diary design.
Standardised the quoting process. Templated output, defined follow-up windows, and tracked conversion per engineer for the first time. Made the invisible visible.
Built the customer journey from scratch. Booking confirmation, pre-visit comms, and post-job review prompt all automated and consistent, regardless of who was in the office that day.
Created the ops dashboard. Single view of utilisation, live pipeline, and satisfaction score. Used in the weekly ops review from day one, not month three.
04
What I would look at first at MJP.
Before any new hire or system investment, these three audits show where the biggest gains are.
Week 1
Diary Audit
Map actual utilisation versus booked capacity per engineer. Identify recurring white space patterns by day of week, job type, and area. Sets the baseline.
Week 2
Quote Conversion Audit
Identify the drop-off point and follow-up gaps. Pull the last three months of quotes, map each outcome, and find the stage where revenue is being lost silently.
Week 3
Customer Journey Map
Walk through the current experience from first enquiry to post-job review. Find where consistency breaks down by job type and who is responsible for each touchpoint today.
